# OBSERVATIONS

## Phase 1 – Business Understanding

### Observation 001
The warehouse handles multiple categories of consumer goods rather than a single product line. Categories: foods, beverages, personal care products, and household.

### Observation 002
Pain points:  
- Delayed Delivery = Expected Delivery Date - Delivery Date
- Incomplete Orders = Order Status
- Receiving Incorrect Products = Items Returned Labelled Incorrect Product

### Observation 003
The warehouse network consists of one central warehouse and three regional warehouses.

### Observation 004
The business serves B2B customers, including retailers, supermarkets, wholesalers, and online retailers.  
Customer Categories = retailers, supermarkets, wholesalers, online retailers

### Observation 005
Avialable Storage Space = Total Storage Space = Orders Dispatched.  
Available Storage Category Wise = Total Storage Space Category Wise - Orders Dispatched Category wise

### Observation 005
The major business problems identified are:
- Stockouts of fast-moving products.
- Overstock of slow-moving products.
- Delayed order processing.
- Inventory count mismatches.
Metrics = Stocks, 
Stock Status = Goods received - Goods dispatched (Itemwise, Category Wise, Total Stock Status)

### Observation 006
Customer dissatisfaction is primarily caused by delayed deliveries, incomplete orders, and incorrect shipments.  
Stock Status will answer the question.

### Observation 007
The client's primary business objective is to improve product availability while reducing excess inventory and improving delivery performance.

### Observation 008
Warehouse information is primarily managed through a Warehouse Management System (WMS) integrated with an ERP system, with some reporting performed in Excel.
